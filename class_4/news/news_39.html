IBM公司研究员吴凌飞博士访问我实验室并作高水平学术报告

2019年05月22日

2019 年 5 月 22 日下午，IBM公司 T. J. Watson研究中心AI Foundations实验室研究员吴凌飞博士访问大数据分析与应用安徽省重点实验室，并在中国科大西区科技实验楼 118 会议室作了题为《Graph-to-Sequence Learning in Natural Language Processing》的精彩报告，报告会由大数据分析与应用安徽省重点实验室刘淇副教授主持。科大相关院系感兴趣的师生聆听了本次报告。

本次报告，吴凌飞博士首先从著名的Seq2Seq模型引入主题，介绍了Seq2Seq模型广泛的应用场景，如机器翻译、自然语言生成等，同时也指出模型的主要不足——输入必须是序列、不能处理如图(Graph)等更加复杂的结构等。另一方面，由于图结构本身诸多特性，例如能够很好的表示结点间的复杂关系、其结构化语义信息能辅助增强原序列数据等，使得图结构能够比简单的序列结构更好的承载信息，所以很多时候我们需要直接给模型输入图结构。于是，吴博士引出了本次报告主要讲解的工作——Graph2Seq模型，其本质是一种用于图表示学习的注意力机制序列自编码模型，由图编码器、双向结点嵌入、图嵌入、结点注意力模型、图解码器等部分组成。在介绍完模型后，吴博士重点介绍了两项相关工作，一项是利用Graph2Seq模型实现从SQL语句生成文本，另一项是基于模型并利用丰富的句法信息实现语义解析任务。最后吴博士总结了今天报告的要点，并展望了所处团队正在拓展的两个方向。

报告结束后，吴凌飞博士耐心地回答了会场听众的问题，并耐心地和到场的老师、同学交流科研工作中的一些想法。

报告结束后，刘淇副教授为吴博士颁发了“杰出讲者”奖杯。

首旅集团党委书记、董事长段强致辞

个人简介：Lingfei Wu is a Research Staff Member in the IBM AI Foundations Labs, Reasoning group at IBM T. J. Watson Research Center. He earned his Ph.D degree in computer science from College of William and Mary in August 2016, under the supervision of Prof. Andreas Stathopoulos. His research interests lie at the intersection of Machine Learning (Deep Learning), Representation Learning, and Natural Language Processing, with a particular emphasis on the fast-growing subjects of Graph Neural Networks and its extension on new application domains and tasks. He was the recipients of the Best Paper Award in IEEE ICC2019 and the Best Student Paper Award in AIAED2019. Lingfei’s research has been featured in numerous media outlets, including Venturebeat, TechTalks, Jiqizhixin, MIT News, IBM Research News, and SIAM News. Lingfei has published more than 30 top-ranked conference and journal papers, including but not limited to NIPS, ICML, ICLR, SYSML, AISTATS, KDD, ICDM, NAACL, EMNLP, AAAI, ICASSP, SC, SIAM Journal on Scientific Computing, IEEE Transaction on Big Data, and Journal of Computational Physics. He is also a co-inventor of more than 16 filled US patents. Lingfei organized or served as a Workshop co-chairs of The First International Workshop on Deep Learning on Graphs. Methods and Applications (DLG’ 19), Track co-chairs of The Third International Conference on AI+ Adaptive Education (AIAED’ 19), Tutorial co-chairs of IEEE BigData’ 18. In addition, he has regularly served as a TPC member of many major AI/ML/DL/DM/NLP conferences, including but not limited to NIPS, ICML, ICLR, KDD, ACL, IJCAI, and AAAI.
